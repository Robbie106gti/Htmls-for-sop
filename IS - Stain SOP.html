Sesame

We supply Toner.

Stained but not lacquered.

This is typically used when a customer wants a more durable finish then our std. on a wood countertop. We won’t do the finish because they typically want this scenario with a sink cutout in it which means splashing water, which means we don’t want to get into the warranty issues of future water damage. With some of our stains this is no issue, but with some (Copper, Bronze, Autumn, Chocolate, Nutbrown and some custom colors that will have to individually researched) the finish they apply may react with unknown results to our stain. This is because our 2 step stain process for the listed stains that includes a “stain in lacquer” as step 2. Our solution to this is that we can stain the counter for the customer and prep it for finishing and we recommend they use a Plastofix finish. Available at Chemcraft, the product code is 488-0XX. For more details of this product, contact Paint or OE.

Stain process for all stains except Sesame:
  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. A minimum of 2 passes. This will result in 80% of the color. Brief drying period (15 minutes).
  3. Spray on Spray and Wipe stain and wipe immediately. This will fill in all shadow lines and the stain will soak into the material. Brief drying period (15 minutes minimum).
  4. Seal.
  5. Drying period.
  6. Sand
  7. Topcoat
  8. Drying period.
Stain process for Sesame:
  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. Brief drying period (15 minutes).
  3. Seal.
  4. Drying period.
  5. Sand
  6. Topcoat
  7. Drying period.

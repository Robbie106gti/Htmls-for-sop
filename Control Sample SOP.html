Definition:

A sample of all colors with a heritage distressing (use Grooved sample block), country (use Grooved sample block), glaze (use Grooved sample block), stain and any exotic (yes, even natural because the exotic material dye lots may change) applications, for all the orders that we produce.

Purpose:

So we have a sample in house for all recent orders that may need an addition repair.

Parameters:

It will be 6“W x 13“H x 5/8” thick veneered K3 unless it is a heritage distressing, country or glazed finish when we will use 13” long (use Grooved sample block). The material will become MDF if it is a painted with glaze application. It will have the color, date and order # and gun/pump settings written on the back. The order # will also be written on one of the 6” edges.

Managing the Control Sample:

  1. E will add this “Control Sample” as a separate line in Quoin to all orders that have colors with a drag glaze and stained applications. Only one Control Sample is required for all individual colors that fall into these parameters, per order. Put the control sample into the first room that the finish appears.

  2. B will cut along with all other parts.

  3. PA will label the Control Sample and supply it to F along with all other parts.

  4. F will finish the Control Sample along with the other parts.

  5. QC will put the Control Sample in the Control Sample rack in numerical order (left to right, from top to bottom).

  6. QC will remove all Control Samples from the Control Sample rack after they are more than 1 year old.

Using a Control Sample:

When O needs a color sample for a repair to an order that is less than 1 year old, they will:

  1. When entering the order in Quoin, choose “Control Sample” as the color and in the room info area, note the control sample job# and item number.

  2. F will get the sample when required and send it along with the order.

The goal behind these changes is to further reduce reworking of custom cabinets and to reduce errors.

Custom sketch:
These are those hand drawings you see in the middle of a shop plan.

  • Front of cross sections/side views are to the right.

  • Scale = 1mm = 1”

  • Dimensions = metric. Put on top and front of cross section sketches.

  • Height dimensions should be per section and to the center of the dowel hole. See example. Feel free to have dimensions between ROS boxes, but they still need the distance between the holes.

  • Write door heights and extra items in the appropriate section if there is room.

  • Adj. shelves are a single dashed line.

Custom drawing:
These are those AutoCAD drawings you see with a shop plan.

  • Sizes will be in metric.

  • Front of cross sections/side views are to the right.

  • Standard measurements aren’t required. eg. If a mounting plate is in a standard position, the measurement isn’t required.

  • Dimensions will be “hook to hole” (from end of part to middle of a hole so a measuring tape can hook over the end of a part before assembly).

  • Door and frame measurements will be on the right hand side.

  • Cabinet and shelf placement sizes will be on the left.

  • Custom hole/item placement will be in the cabinet.

  • If the cabinet has more then strictly moving parts up and down, then all parts will be drawn on an individual page with holes dowels and routes along with their dimensions. The individual parts should al have their own name and the name should be on the drawings. eg. HFU’s will have all parts individually printed along with the standard drawings.

  • These drawings will be with both shop copies directly behind the shop summary sheets before the actual shop item list.

Rustic Finish

Applying the distressing technique(s) to be after the seal coat and before the top coat.
Description :

A new finish process that is linked with our distressing techniques (Worm Holes, Worn Corners, Dents & Dings, Rasping or Aged Finish) and defines the point of applying the distressing technique(s) to be after the seal coat and before the top coat.

Example: Knotty Alder/ Cognac / Rustic Worm Holes/Drag-Ebony/Satin

Steps to achieve this example :

After the seal coat and before the final topcoat of any of our standard finishing processes, the Glazing team will apply a combination of Worm Holes to the corners/edges and front face of all solid wood material on an order. They will then apply a drag glaze, and follow that with the top coat.

  1. Stain
  2. Seal
  3. Sand
  4. Seal
  5. Worm holes
  6. Ebony Drag
  7. Top coat
Rules :

This finish will be applied to raised panel doors, to the solid frame area of a flat panel door, to overlay fillers, and to solid wood appliance frames. Mouldings, finish ends, baseboards, and any other veneer panels including the flat panels on doors will get an application of Dents & Dings but Nickels will not apply Worm Holes, Worn Corners or Rasping to any of these parts.

We want to eliminate nailing strips on header returns, gable fillers etc.:
  1. so installers don’t need to worry about cutting into staples or
  2. Stop the nailing strip from being in the way.:

Use the Spluit joint for Header return scenarios and use biscuits for larger situations… eg. a tall pantry gable filler.

Using the Spluit joint

  • Garett tested it and it works like a charm for mouldings with returns. For wider items, we'll continue to use biscuits.

  • 1 biscuit every 10" to 12". For melamines and laminates, use a full length spline.

  • 15 min. / piece and dry time

  1. Put Spluit blade into saw
  2. Adjust height using Spluit height gauge. Width = 6mm.
  3. Mark parts so you know faces you're working on
  4. Break edges (microbevel)
  5. Notch appropriate parts.
  6. Put glue in grooves and back shoulder of the return.
  7. Insert biscuits every 10" to 12" and join.
  8. Clamp and let dry.
  9. Unclamp and stack

Purpose:

Used to cover the joint between the door insert and the door frame on the back of a door. When there is an insert with a leaded or brass border, the CSM is used to fill the gap between the insert border and the door frame.

Standards:

  • Elias doors use the plastic insert that their frames are grooved for. For all others we do the following.

  • Wood available in up to 10’ lengths.

  • Door team supplies to Finishing team when required. Amount required = (rabbet height x 2) + (rabbet width x 2) + 305mm/12”.

  • Finish 3 sides of the CSM except for DM-DWM and DM-SWM applications.

  • Finish 4 sides of the CSM for DM-DWM and DM-SWM (wire mesh) inserts. The D team will notify F if 4 sides need to be finished.

  • For speaker cloth applications the D team will cut the CSM width, 1mm less than the height of the rabbet.

  • Plymouth and Brunswick door, only when ordered from Maplecraft… The 11mm width changes to 22mm.

  • Glass doors with an arch. The back of the door in the arched area will be made square (do in house if the door company doesn’t already do it.) so that we can order a square piece of glass and use our wood cover strips to cover the back edges. If the glass door has a brass or leaded edge, the back of the door won’t be made for a square glass insert. We will have to get a template made for the glass supplier and we will then have to make a custom glass CSM (cover strip moulding) on the P2P machine.

  • We will send the CSM (cover strips) for situations when the door inserts are by owner. They will be cut to size in our shop and sent along with the cabinets to be installed by the owner if they so desire.

  • S will label excess CSM with the color and return it to the D Team.

  • With mullions and 1 piece of glass over entire door:

    • No CSM is required for the mullions.

    • Where mullions cross, just put 1 drop of silicone in the center.

    • For single mullions, just put 1 drop of silicone in the center if required.

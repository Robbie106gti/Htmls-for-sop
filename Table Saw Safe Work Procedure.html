PERSONAL PROTECTION EQUIPMENT (PPE)

remove_red_eye

Safety glasses or face shield must be worn at all times in work areas and specifically when operating a portable circular saw.

face

Long and loose hair must be tied back.

Appropriate footwear must be worn when preforming work with table saws. Shoe must be fully enclosed. No open toed shoes allowed.

accessibility

Close fitting/protective clothing must be worn. Remove strings hanging from pullovers/sweaters.

block

Rings and jewelry (long necklaces / bracelets, etc.) must not be worn. Lighters, matches and cell phones are not allowed in the shop area when the shop is operational. Wearing gloves is allowed when using table saws, if desired by operator.

hearing

Hearing protection must be worn as per shop location protocols and when using this machine constantly for an extended period of time. Hearing protection is always recommended when using table saw equipment.

Other PPE Considerations

Dust masks may be required depending on material being worked on and potential worker sensitivities.

HAZARDS PRESENT
SUPPORTING REQUIREMENTS

· Discharge of objects or materials from cutting process

· Saw blade binding causing kickback or violent movement of the saw or materials

· Serious cut and amputation potential to hands, fingers or other body parts from saw blade

· Eye injury potential from discharged objects or materials from cutting process

· Respiratory risks when material being cut become airborne (sensitivities)

· Potential awkward postures when positioning to use the saw

· Noise exposure from prolonged period of equipment use or workspace environment.

· Metal friction heating at point of operation (blade and material potentially)

· People talking too/distracting the operator.

· Operator being distracted / talking to others, or turning their face away from the blade.

· General shops and department safety orientations

· Read and understand general safety rules and regulations

SAFE WORK PROCEDURE

DO NOT use this machine or perform this process unless you have been instructed in safe procedures / operation and you have been given authorization to perform associated tasks.

Always use tools and equipment for their intended purposes only.

Always take out of service and report damaged equipment to your Team Leader without delay.

Pre-Job Safety:

  1. Inspect table saw and blades, looking for any damage. Ensure that the table saw has a guard with a spreader and anti-kickback fingers and that it is functioning properly. Do not operate table saw unless adequately guarded.

  2. Know where the emergency stop button/switch is prior to using the table saw.

  3. Ensure local exhaust ventilation and or dust collection is on or wear recommended respiratory protection.

    4. Set Up:

  4. Install/replace blade/s; Ensure lockout switch is in the off position. Install a saw blade that is the correct size and RPM rating. Ensure that saw blade direction is correct. Replace saw blades that are dull or damaged. Keep saw blades clean. Use correct tool for installing/adjusting saw blade. If unsure of any procedure/s ask instructor. Blade should not be too high above the material. For standard cutting, the amount of the blade that is above the material should be no more than 1/8” above the bottom of the gullet.

  5. Transport work piece to table saw. Remember proper lifting techniques, ask for assistance from co-worker. Ensure route to table saw is clear of debris and clutter.

  6. Set up work piece; provide adequate support to the rear and sides of the table saw for wide or long work pieces. Inspect work piece for nails or other foreign materials before cutting/ripping. Rip cuts less than 1 inch should never be attempted.

  7. Remove any keys/wrenches that were used for adjusting/installing saw blade/s prior to pushing start button.

  8. Ensure work area is clean and clear of any debris prior to pushing start button. Put on personal protective equipment.

  9. Open the Vacuum system for wood and composite core items. Close the vacuum for metal items.

    10. Operate / Perform Process:

  10. Ensure that stock/work piece is away from blade before pushing the start button. Let saw blade get to full speed before cutting/ripping stock/work piece.

  11. Keep hands/fingers at a safe distance and out of the line of the saw blade, (line of cut). Use a push stick for rip cuts between 1 and 5 inches.

  12. Avoid performing “free hand” sawing operations by holding stock firmly against the miter gauge or rip fence to position and guide the cut.

  13. Keep body/face to one side of the line of the saw blade, (line of cut) and slowly feed stock/work piece into and against the direction of the saw blade rotation. Do not apply too much pressure against saw blade.

  14. Jammed stock/work piece; Push machine stop button and wait for the saw blades to come to a complete stop. Turn the lockout switch to the off position, then free stock/work piece.

    15. Post Job Procedure:

  15. Clean up area around table saw, removing any tools and debris. Return tools to proper storage area. Inform Team Leader of any damaged tools or of any damage to table saw and or blade/s. Cover blade.

Lockout Procedure:

  1. Isolate electrical power from saw (plug end or breaker)

  2. Apply appropriate lockout device at energy isolation point

  3. Apply locks, signs and tags as per established lockout procedures. Use the “Under maintenance” sign if you leave the machine unattended.

  4. Ensure that the entire table saw is in a zero energy state prior to performing maintenance.

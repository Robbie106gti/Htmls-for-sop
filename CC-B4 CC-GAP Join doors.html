CC-GAP

Joining of doors and/or drawer fronts with a 3mm gap. We will be using this for our sample door/drawer combos.

Painted application

  • Use a 3mm thick x 13mm wide wood spacer.

  • 6mm inset on the sides.

  • Flush on the back

  • Attach to one of the doors and drill and paint along with the door.

Use the 50 mm long x 8mm diameter dowels.

Unpainted application

  • Use a 3mm thick x 13mm black plastic spacer.

  • 6mm inset on the sides.

  • Flush on the back

Use the 50 mm long x 8mm diameter dowels.

The D team :

  • Attach the wood spacer to one of the items.

  • The items are drilled on the Dowel Inserter.

  • For our sample door drawer setup (12”wide), use 4 dowels. For wider doors/drawers, use more dowels if required.

  • Insert the dowels after finishing.

DP team :

  • Join the appropriate parts.

  • Make sure when you are joining 5 piece doors, that if there are dowels beyond the end rails and there is any potential of the rails bending, you use a clamp on the back when possible to help keep the rails straight.

When drilling Parapan or Acrylic doors , extra steps are required on the Dowel inserter:

  1. Slow the horizontal drilling by lowering the air pressure of the horizontal drilling by lowering the pressure valve screw as much as possible.

  2. You must drill the door in to stages. Activate the drilling by pressing on the foot pedal but hit the emergency stop button when the drills just start drilling into the door. Reset the emergency stop button. Reactivate the drilling by pressing on the foot pedal and let it drill completely into the door this time.

  3. When all drilling is complete, reset the pressure valve screw by raising it until it is again level with the other pressure valve screw (for vertical drilling).

CC-B4

  • The joined parts are pushed completely together, no gap.

  • Use the 50 mm long x 8mm diameter dowels.

The D team :

  • The items are drilled on the Dowel Inserter.

  • For our sample door drawer setup (12”wide), use 4 dowels. For wider doors/drawers, use more dowels if required.

  • For 5 piece doors: The first dowel from both edges is centered at 20mm and the second at 52mm. (This is to keep the dowels on the vertical rails and to avoid any potential horizontal rail bending). If more dowels are required, use as few as possible. The more dowels that you have, the harder it is to join the doors and it is also more likely to cause bending of the rail. If unsure, ask your team head for help.

  • For slab doors: The first dowel from both edges is centered at 20mm and the second at 84mm.

  • When moving drills on the dowel inserter, always add a drop of oil to the shaft of the drill bit.

  • Insert the dowels after finishing.

DP team :

  • Same rules as CC-GAP apply.

  • When drilling Parapan or Acrylic doors, the same rules as CC-GAP apply.

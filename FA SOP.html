Amount of employees required:

  • T cab’s = 2 people in cell 1.

  • BC’s, BAC’s, WC’s, WAC’s, Custom (angled) cab’s = 1 person in cell 1.

  • Base and Walls = 3 people in cell 1.

Standard procedures:

  • Beginning of the day:

    • Check that all screw and staple supplies are stocked up for the day.

    • Take enough hubs for the day.

  • For each job:

    • Collect all required hardware.

    • Check that all finished parts are there.

    • Put adj. shelf and position 4 parts (headers, stiles, hardware) onto position 3 dolly.

  • Daily after 2:00:

    • Check that all hardware has been ordered for new jobs (Check Shipping copy’s).

Cabinet assembly order:

  1. Cabinets that require position 4 (DD gables, stiles, garbage cans, AD’s, cutting boards etc.) attention.

  2. T

  3. BC’s, BAC’s, WC’s, WAC’s, Custom (angled).

  4. Base

  5. Walls

Position 1 :

  • Collect parts.

  • Label backing.

  • Attach hardware. Use 4 screws for all slides longer then 15” and 3 screws for the rest.

  • Lean Backing and Bottom against bench.

  • Stack Gables and Stretchers.

Position 2 :

  • Glue

  • Assemble

  • Mallet

  • Staple

  • Tack on backing.

  • Clamp.

  • Fasten backing.

Position 3 :

  • Put call board in place.

  • Clean.

  • Remove from Clamp.

  • Install Adj.

  • Put any position 4 parts onto conveyor/cabinet.

Position 4 :

  • Attach DD gables, stiles, garbage cans, AD’s, cutting boards etc.

Position 5 :

  • Install drawer boxes, drawer fronts and doors.

Position 6 :

  • Package cabinet.

Position 7 :

  • Wrap and palletize Cabinets.

Position 8 :

Package accessories.

Position 9 :

Wrap pallets with Cabinets and accessories.

Standards :

Flush fixed shelf and gable joints. The thickness of the edge tape is the maximum overhang allowed.

When a cabinet seems to be incorrect and isn’t making sense:

  1. The assembler and the Team Head will try to solve the problem within a reasonable amount of time (the reasonable amount of time is to be determined by the Team head).

  2. The Team Head will call the engineer who processed the cabinet to the assembly dept. if they can’t solve the problem on their own.

  3. When a solution is found and if new parts are needed from Breakout, the Breakout Team Head will determine whether it is better to optimize the parts or cut them manually.

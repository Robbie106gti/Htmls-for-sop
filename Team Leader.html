Team Leader Job Description

A team leader at Nickels is directly responsible for the area in which he/she has been assigned. Because of the nature of this position it is imperative that this person be in agreement with and actively promotes all of Nickels policies, procedures and initiatives. Team leaders are directly accountable to the plant manager.

General Responsibilities:
  1. PERSONNEL:

    • Monitor and support the performance of assigned staff on an on-going basis.

    • Keep all work cells in your area working efficiently according to SOP’s.

    • Train & evaluate new employees at (3), (6), (12) & (24) months in order to assimilate into team.

    • Recommend changes as needed to plant manager (hiring, lay-offs, promotions, discipline, etc.).

    • Promote an atmosphere of harmony and deal swiftly with internal conflict. If unable to resolve conflict, let plant manager know and together come to resolution.

    • Cross-train employees within your own team and other teams to alleviate schedule constraints and worker absence.

    • Identify and train (1) employee in your area to take over your responsibilities during your absence.

  2. SCHEDULE:

    • Plan workload (3) days in advance to anticipate potential changes in order to meet schedule.

    • Update tracking status in Quoin daily between 2:45 and 3:00.

    • Update daily output totals in Tracking Parts excel file between 2:45 and 3:00.

    • Prioritize workload according to Quoin schedule & assign specific job duties.

    • Communicate with plant manager & other team leaders to obtain cross-trained employees when unable to meet schedule.

    • Assign overtime as needed in order to maintain schedule.

    • Notify plant manager if unable to meet scheduled deadlines.

  3. COMMUNICATION:

    • Maintain clear communication with other team leaders and plant manager.

    • Obtain information and offer constructive feedback at team leader meetings.

    • Relay information and obtain feedback to and from assigned team.

    • Clearly post all applicable SOP’s and ensure team members are following them; stopping the process when SOP’s are not being followed. Update SOP’s when changes have been made.

  4. QUALITY CONTROL:

    • -Train employees to maintain a consistent level of quality in accordance with Nickels standards.

    • -Clearly post standards (acceptable vs. not acceptable) together with SOP’s.

    • -Organize and maintain work area in accordance with 5S principles.

    • -Work in conjunction with other team leaders to ensure our standards are maintained on a company wide basis.

  5. SAFETY:

    • Instruct subordinates on proper use of machinery and support safety committee in establishing and maintaining a safe working environment.

  6. RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT:

    • Work closely with lean implementation team to improve efficiency and quality and when new products are introduced to be brought to the marketplace.

  7. PROBLEM SOLVING:

    In order to keep the work flowing steadily, any and all problems arising in your assigned area are your direct responsibility to solve. Solving problems does not necessarily mean fixing them, but it may include this activity as well.

  8. HANDS ON WORK:
    • When all above responsibilities have been taken care of, you are required to work on the floor, always aware of the goings-on in your area.

SOP

  • Keep all the work cells within your team working as efficiently as possible, following the SOP’s and working on the appropriate order(s).

  • Be aware of the production schedule and keep your team on schedule. If you are off of the schedule, inform scheduling immediately so they can adjust accordingly.

  • Every day, between 2:45 and 3:00, all Team leaders are to have their Tracking Status in Quoin updated. See tracking Status SOP for details.

  • Every day, between 2:45 and 3:00, all Team are to have their daily output updated.

  • Keep your entire Team informed of what’s going on with SOP’s etc.

  • Post all required SOP’s and ensure that all are following them.

  • Updating SOP’s when necessary.

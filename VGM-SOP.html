VGM (Vertical Grain Match)

HGM (Horizontal Grain Match)

Also see the HGM SOP

This means that all doors and/or drawers on an individual cabinet will be cut from the same piece of material (when possible) and installed in the same order that they were on the original material. Side by side doors will match.


Breakout:
Preload:

  • Use Sequence matched sheets whenever possible.

  • Use the layout reports to optimize material.

  • Do the Doors first.

Panel saw:

  • Consistently cut the material in the appropriate, same direction, so the cathedral is up.

  • Mark all parts with the appropriate item number and joining lines. Mark using a pencil with veneer and a wax stick/crayon with melamines.

  • Keep off-cuts labelled until order is shipped.

Edgebander:

  • Try to maintain the sequence of parts so they don’t get mixed up.

Door team:

  • Check doors for standard things along with grain matching.

  • Stamp all doors to indicate cabinet and positioning:

    • Doors –Stamp on the back, hinge side, top.

    • Drawers –Stamp on the back, center, bottom.

  • All melamine doors will have crayon/wax cleaned off by the door team after they stamp the match numbers on the doors.

Finishing team:

  • Separate the doors using the Layout sheets and mark them on the door racks so they can be finished per wall/run.

  • After staining, put them on the light wall to check colour match.

Door Preparation team:

  • Ensure that they are put on the correct cabinet and that each cabinets grain matching is correct.

Cabinet wrapping team:

  • Ensure that each cabinets grain matching is correct.

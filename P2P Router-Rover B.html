Goals:

Keep the router constantly working.

Supply customers with what they need and how they need it.

The material should remain in good condition.

Job Priority:

Take the next batch from the panel saw.

Procedure:
  • Scan a maximum of 5 pieces at a time.
  • The piece must be against the machine pins.
  • After machine starts running on a piece, check the computer screen and make sure the next program is correct.
  • If a custom program is needed, leave it for Engineering to program and machine.
  • If something seems wrong with a program, check plan and see what it say’s. If the piece needs to be re-cut, notify appropriate people. If custom programming is required, put the piece aside and start another. Notify Engineering when appropriate.
  • Quality check part. Chipping means that a tool needs to be replaced and sent for sharpening.
  • Rack part according to the following Customer needs.
Custom cabinets:
  • Dry fit all angled custom cabinets and check that all machining is correct and all parts are present.
  • Check that all machining is correct and all parts are present for all custom cabinets that aren’t angled. Nothing can be assumed as standard.
Customers needs:
When machining Finished Parts:

Separate by:

  1. Job number
  2. Edge tape color
  3. Wall gables-keep the taped edge down.
  4. Material thickness
  5. Always leave at least a 2” minimum space in the rack. No stacking. Never 1 behind the other.
When machining Unfinished Parts:

Separate by:

  1. Job number
  2. Wall gables-keep the taped edge down.
  3. Material thickness
  4. Try to keep like pieces together as much as possible. e.g. All stretchers on the end. Etc.
  5. Always leave at least a 1” minimum space in the rack. No stacking. Never 1 behind the other.
Operator Notes:
  • When using a tool from the Rover A, they are 0.4mm smaller.
  • Horizontal holes:

    8mm Diameter
    20, 64, 96, 160, 96
    25mm deep.

  • Vertical holes:

    8mm Diameter
    20, 64, 96, 160, 96
    12mm deep.

Change Bit:
  • Scan the unload program.
  • Run it in the left field.
  • Get the chuck and remove the blades.
  • Clean the area with a wire brush.
  • Note that the blades are to be installed as far down as possible.
  • DON”T OVER TIGHTEN.
  • Replace the chuck.

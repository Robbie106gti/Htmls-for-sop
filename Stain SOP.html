Stain processes. In depth descriptions:

Stain process for Sesame:

  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. A minimum of 2 passes.
  3. Seal
  4. Drying period
  5. Sand
  6. Topcoat
  7. Drying period

Stain process for all stains except Sesame:

  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. A minimum of 2 passes. This will result in 80% of the color. Brief drying period (15 minutes).
  3. Spray on Spray and Wipe stain and wipe immediately. This will fill in all shadow lines and the stain will soak into the material. Brief drying period (15 minutes minimum).
  4. Seal.
  5. Drying period.
  6. Sand
  7. Topcoat
  8. Drying period.

Stain process for all stains, except Sesame, with an eco finish:

  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. A minimum of 2 passes. This will result in 80% of the color. Brief drying period (15 minutes).
  3. Spray on Spray and Wipe stain and wipe immediately. This will fill in all shadow lines and the stain will soak into the material. Brief drying period (15 minutes minimum).
  4. Seal front and back with Eco lacquer.
  5. Drying period.
  6. Sand
  7. Topcoat front and back with Eco lacquer.
  8. Drying period.

Stain process for all stains, except Sesame, with an eco finish and a glaze:

  1. Sand
  2. Spray on toner. A minimum of 2 passes. This will result in 80% of the color. Brief drying period (15 minutes).
  3. Spray on Spray and Wipe stain and wipe immediately. This will fill in all shadow lines and the stain will soak into the material. Brief drying period (15 minutes minimum).
  4. Seal front and back.
  5. Drying period.
  6. Sand
  7. Topcoat front and back.
  8. Drying period.
  9. Drag glaze front and edges.
  10. Topcoat front and back with Eco lacquer.

Stained but not lacquered

This is typically used when a customer wants a more durable finish then our std. on a wood countertop. We won’t do the finish because they typically want this scenario with a sink cutout in it which means splashing water, which means we don’t want to get into the warranty issues of future water damage. With some of our stains this is no issue, but with some (Copper, Bronze, Autumn, Chocolate, Nutbrown and some custom colors that will have to individually researched) the finish they apply may react with unknown results to our stain. This is because our 2 step stain process for the listed stains that includes a “stain in lacquer” as step 2. Our solution to this is that we can stain the counter for the customer and prep it for finishing and we recommend they use a Plastofix finish. Available at Chemcraft, the product code is 488-0XX. For more details of this product, contact Paint or OE.

Exotic materials

The Exotic materials are still available in a Natural finish. This is because they are engineered and they are extremely consistent in act wood shade and color unlike un-engineered species. These exotic materials will require a washcoat to seal off the open grain when they have a Natural finish. EW RC White Oak, EW QC Walnut, EW QC Macassar Ebony, EW QC Zebrawood, EW QC Teak, EW QC Mahogany, EW QC Wenge, EW QC Gun Metal Ebony.

Old stain steps:

Application for Chocolate and Bronze:

  1. Apply the stain by wiping on with a rag.
  2. Seal with lacquer.
  3. Sand.
  4. Tone with 4/1 ratio of lacquer/stain.
  5. Top coat with lacquer.

Application for Amber:

  1. Apply the stain by spraying 4/1 ratio of lacquer/stain.
  2. Sealer
  3. Sand
  4. Top coat with lacquer.

Application for Copper:

  1. Apply the stain by wiping on with a rag.
  2. Seal with 20/1 ratio of lacquer/stain.
  3. Sand.
  4. Top coat with lacquer.

Application for all other wipe on stains (eg. Cognac):

  1. Apply the stain by wiping on with a rag.
  2. Seal lacquer.
  3. Sand.
  4. Top coat with lacquer.

Application for all other spray on stains (eg. Nut Brown):

  1. Apply the stain by spraying on.
  2. Seal lacquer.
  3. Sand.
  4. Top coat with lacquer.

Square:

Warpage:

We have stricter standards then the CKCA. Doors less than 812mm (32”) in height shall have a maximum deflection of 2mm (CKCA=2.5mm (3/32”)). Any door greater than 812mm (32”) in height shall have a maximum deflection of 3.5mm (CKCA=5mm (3/16”)).

Doors for the Aventos setups cannot have any warping.

Glass doors with an arch.

The back of the door in the arched area will be made square (do in house if the door company doesn’t already do it.) so that we can order a square piece of glass and use our wood cover strips to cover the back edges. If the glass door has a brass or leaded edge, the back of the door won’t be made for a square glass insert. We will have to get a template made for the glass supplier and we will then have to make a custom glass CSM (cover strip moulding) on the P2P machine.

AD (Applied Door):
Engineering-

If a door doesn’t need to be finished on the back, a label will be supplied to the Door team for it. It will say AD on it.

Door Team and Door Prep Team

No drilling or hinging is required. Put the AD label (supplied by engineering) in the center of the back of all these doors so the Finishing team knows that they only need to put 1 coat of lacquer on the back.

Finishing Team

No paint or stain required on the back of the door. Only put 1 coat of lacquer on the back of the door.

DR (Applied Drawer Front):
Engineering

If a drawer front doesn’t need to be finished on the back, a label will be supplied to the Door team for it. It will say DR on it.

Door Team and Door Prep Team

No drilling or hardware is required. Put the DR label (supplied by engineering) in the center of the back of all these drawer fronts so the Finishing team knows that they only need to put 1 coat of lacquer on the back.

Finishing Team

No paint or stain required on the back of the drawer front. Only put 1 coat of lacquer on the back of the drawer front.

ADP (Applied Door Proud):
Engineering

If a door only needs to be partially finished on the back, a label will be supplied to the Door team for it. It will say ADP on it.

Door Team and Door Prep Team

No drilling or hinging is required. Put the ADP label (supplied by engineering) in the center of the back of all these doors so the Finishing team knows that they only need to finish the outside 2 to 4 inches on the back.

Finishing Team

Paint or stain only the outside rails or the outside 3”, on the back of the door. Lacquer must be put on the entire back of the door.

Door Team Steps
Doors made in house.

  1. Mark as started in Quoin.

  2. Check for deficiencies.

  3. Edge band if required.

  4. Bring to D shop.

  5. Drill and put them in a box. VGM and HGM orders must have the doors stacked in the box in the order of a per cabinet basis. This is to create a better opportunity of the finish matching on each cabinet because the parts will then be finished one immediately after the other.

  6. Deal with all custom scenarios.

  7. Bring to F shop.

  8. Mark as done in Quoin.

Doors made by Maplecraft and Delta Door.

Note that these are usually received on a large skid, hundreds of doors with all the orders mixed to achieve good stacking.

  1. Mark as started in Quoin.

  2. Bring to D shop.

  3. Check for deficiencies and size while separating into individual orders.

  4. Drill and put them in a box.

  5. Deal with all custom scenarios.

  6. Bring to F shop.

  7. Mark as done in Quoin.

Doors made by all others, except Maplecraft and Delta Door.

  1. Mark as started in Quoin.

  2. Bring to D shop.

  3. Check for deficiencies.

  4. Drill and put them in a box.

  5. Deal with all custom scenarios.

  6. Bring to F shop.

  7. Mark as done in Quoin.

Numbering of doors and drawer fronts:

When the doors/drawer fronts are grain and/or sequence matched, they will have their corresponding cabinet number stamped on to the back. The location of the number is:

  • Doors- On the back, at the top and on the hinge side.

  • Drawer Fronts- On the back, at the center of the bottom.

    • Hinge hole depth = 12.7mm.

The piston setup will be used for:

  1. Cabinets with Metro doors.

  2. Triangle cabinets.

  • We are using a new piston with a plastic head instead of a rubber head so the door slides along it better.

  • This piston will now be installed on the hinge side of the cabinet. The amount of pistons to be used are:

    • 1 at the top of wall cabinets.

    • 1 at the bottom of base cabinets.

    • 1 at the top and 1 at the bottom of Tall cabinets.

    • If one piston is insufficient, use 2. The hinge needs to be adjusted out to allow the door to close all the way. All of these applications must be tested to ensure that they are working properly.

A 10mm diameter by 51mm deep hole is needed for the piston to be installed into at 54 mm from inside of the gable.

  • hinge drilling distance = 62.5mm from the end of door to the center of the hole, when door height is under 397mm high

Hinge change to Hinges with the Soft closing action built in.-Sept. 1, 2011

We are changing our hinges to hinges that have the Soft closing action mechanism incorporated into the hinge. This means that we need:

  • More depth (12.5mm) for the hinge.

  • 15mm from the bottom of the door to the start of the profile for these hinges to work.

This affects 2 of our current door edge profiles. The CA edge and the C edge. (see images)
